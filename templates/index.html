Detection of Diabetic Retinopathy using DenseNet-121

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) is a serious eye disease caused by diabetes. Early detection is important to prevent permanent vision loss.

This system uses the DenseNet-121 deep learning model to analyze retinal images and classify them into five stages of Diabetic Retinopathy. It helps support early diagnosis and better patient care.

Upload Retina Image

Understanding DR Stages

The system allows users to upload retina images through a simple interface, and it processes them to classify the condition into one of five stages based on the severity of Diabetic Retinopathy.

No DR

No signs of Diabetic Retinopathy detected. The retina appears healthy with normal blood vessels.

Mild

Minor microaneurysms present. Early-stage DR with minimal retinal changes.

Moderate

More pronounced retinal changes including hemorrhages and lipid deposits.

Severe

Significant retinal damage with extensive hemorrhages and venous beading.

Proliferative

Advanced stage with new blood vessel growth and risk of vitreous hemorrhage.
